## About Me

I'm an engineering student exploring the intersection of **technical excellence** and **user-centric product thinking**. My journey is a deliberate pivot from a traditional engineering mindset to understanding *why* we build—not just *how* we build it.

I believe the best technical products emerge when engineers understand customer problems, business constraints, and design tradeoffs. My goal is to become a **Technical Product Manager** who can bridge engineering and product strategy.

---

## My Approach

###  From Engineering → Product Thinking

Currently, I'm **reframing my projects** with product-first questions:
- **What customer problem does this solve?**
- **Why would users care?**
- **What metrics would prove success?**
- **How do technical constraints shape the product roadmap?**

Rather than building features in isolation, I'm learning to:
- Understand user needs before coding
- Think about scalability, performance, and business viability
- Consider product-market fit from day one
- Document decisions and tradeoffs

## Why TPM?

As someone with a technical foundation, I see **Technical Product Management** as the natural evolution. TPMs understand:
- Deep technical constraints and tradeoffs
- How engineering decisions impact product roadmaps
- The balance between perfection and shipping

I want to be the bridge between what's technically possible and what users actually need.
